Business Intelligence

Key Findings

01 Discount Impact
The Discount Trap
Discounts above 40% consistently produce negative profit. Higher discounts attract more orders but destroy margins.
r = -0.22 (Pearson, p < 0.001)
02 Category Analysis
Technology Dominates
Technology generates ~50% of total profit at only 36% of revenue. Copiers and Phones are the most profitable sub-categories.
Furniture Tables = net loss
03 Regional Analysis
West vs Central Gap
West region leads all metrics. Central region has the lowest profit margins, suggesting operational inefficiency or pricing issues.
Chi-sq: p = 0.005 (Ship Mode)
04 Customer Behaviour
Retention Drops Fast
RFM cohort analysis reveals that customer retention drops sharply after Month 1. Re-engagement strategies are critical for long-term revenue.
RFM: Champions identified
Statistical Validation

Hypothesis Testing Results

Shapiro-Wilk Test
H₀: Profit is normally distributed
Reject H₀
W = 0.2492 | p < 0.001
Profit has heavy skew — use non-parametric methods
Independent T-Test
H₀: Consumer = Corporate profit margin
Fail to Reject H₀
t = -0.846 | p = 0.397
No significant margin difference between segments
One-Way ANOVA
H₀: Sales equal across all regions
Fail to Reject H₀
F = 0.801 | p = 0.493
Sales volumes not statistically different by region
Chi-Square Test
H₀: Ship Mode independent of Region
Reject H₀
χ² = 23.85 | p = 0.005
Shipping preferences depend on the region
