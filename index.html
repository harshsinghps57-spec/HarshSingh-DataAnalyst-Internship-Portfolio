Key Learnings

What I Learned

Summarised technical and professional takeaways from 5 weeks of hands-on data analytics work.

🧹
Data Quality is Everything
Real-world data is messy. I learned that 80% of analytics time goes to cleaning and validating data. A clean dataset is the foundation of every trustworthy insight.
🔍
EDA Reveals Hidden Patterns
Exploratory analysis on the Superstore dataset revealed that deep discounts consistently destroy profit margins — a finding invisible without systematic analysis of distributions and correlations.
📐
Statistics Validates Intuition
Hypothesis tests (ANOVA, Chi-Square, T-Test) gave scientific rigour to visual observations. I learned that intuition must be verified with p-values before acting on business decisions.
📖
Data Needs a Story
Numbers alone don't persuade stakeholders. Crafting narratives around data — The Discount Trap, Regional Disparity — makes insights memorable, actionable and impactful.
🎛️
Dashboards Enable Decisions
Interactive dashboards with live filters empower non-technical stakeholders to explore data themselves. The best analysis is one that others can self-serve without asking the analyst.
🚀
Build, Ship, Iterate
Weekly deliverables taught me the power of consistent output. Each week's work built on the previous, demonstrating how an analytics pipeline evolves from raw data to polished portfolio.
